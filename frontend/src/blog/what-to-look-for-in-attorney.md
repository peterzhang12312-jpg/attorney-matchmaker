# What to Look for in a NY/CA Attorney for Your Case Type

Hiring an attorney is one of the most consequential decisions you'll make for your case. Here's what to look for beyond basic credentials.

## 1. Court-Specific Experience

An attorney who has appeared before Judge X in SDNY 50 times knows that judge's preferences, pet peeves, and scheduling habits. This matters more than most people realize.

Ask: "How many cases have you filed in [specific court]?" and "Have you appeared before [judge]?"

## 2. Motion Track Record

For contested matters, look for attorneys who have filed the specific motions your case may require:
- **TRO/PI** — temporary restraining orders and preliminary injunctions (urgent cases)
- **MSJ** — motions for summary judgment (merit-based dispositive motions)
- **MTD** — motions to dismiss (early case resolution)

Our docket intelligence feature shows exactly which motion types each attorney has filed.

## 3. Practice Area Depth

There's a big difference between an attorney who handles real estate cases "among other things" and one whose entire docket is real estate. For complex matters, you want the specialist.

## 4. Budget Transparency

Get hourly rates upfront. Ask about:
- Retainer size and refund policy
- Blended rate vs. partner/associate split
- Flat fee options for predictable phases (pleadings, discovery, trial)

## 5. Availability

An overloaded attorney is a bad attorney for your case. Ask current caseload and expected response time for emails.
